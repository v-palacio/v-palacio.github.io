About Me

Viviana is a postdoctoral researcher in Theoretical Chemistry at UIUC with an interdisciplinary background spanning chemical engineering, materials science, and molecular engineering. She holds a B.S. and M.S. from Universidad Nacional de Colombia and a PhD in Molecular Engineering from the University of Chicago.

Her research harnesses machine learning to enhance multiscale modeling and thermodynamic frameworks for studying soft matter phenomena in and out of equilibrium, particularly in liquid crystals. She focuses on linking molecular structure to mesoscale behavior and optical responses, with strong ties to experimental validation.

Outside of research, she enjoys collecting hobbies—knitting, baking, and exploring molecular gastronomy among her favorites.

Research Interests

  • Machine Learning in Chemistry: Development and application of ML methods for multiscale modeling
  • Soft Matter Physics: Liquid crystals and complex fluids
  • Thermodynamics: Equilibrium and non-equilibrium processes
  • Molecular Engineering: Structure-property relationships
  • Computational Chemistry: Multiscale modeling approaches
